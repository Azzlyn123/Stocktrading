Why I Don’t Think That Lever Is The Real Fix

Look at the structure:
	•	50–60% of trades never reach T1
	•	Time stop WR is ~9–10%
	•	That cohort dominates expectancy

That means the majority of trades are structurally weak.

Letting post-T1 trades run longer doesn’t change the fact that half your trades never show strength.

That’s upstream.

Not downstream.

⸻

The Real Question

Why are so many trades failing to impulse?

Your system should be asking:

If this setup is good… why doesn’t it move immediately?

Strong trades show early intent.

Weak trades drift.

Right now, you’re treating them the same for 45 minutes.

That’s the flaw.

⸻

The Better Path (In My Opinion)

Instead of adjusting the back end (time stop, exemptions, classification)…

Move forward and attack the front end.

Add an early confirmation filter.

Example:

If trade does NOT reach:
	•	+0.15R within first 10 minutes
OR
	•	+0.20R within first 15 minutes

→ Kill it early at small loss.

This does three things:
	1.	Cuts losers faster
	2.	Raises win rate
	3.	Preserves runner logic

Because real runners almost always show early expansion.

⸻

Why This Is More Powerful Than Time Stop Tweaks

Time stop adjustments:
	•	Tweak outcome timing
	•	Do not improve population quality

Impulse filter:
	•	Changes population
	•	Directly increases WR
	•	Improves expectancy structurally

⸻

Important Reality

Your runner capture logic is good.

Your trailing logic is good.

Your issue is trade selection quality.

You don’t need better exits.

You need fewer mediocre entries.

⸻

So What Do I Believe?

I believe:
	•	v6.6 runner logic is solid
	•	The system has a real edge
	•	But it is diluted by too many low-momentum setups

The next real leap is:

Impulse qualification within first 10–15 minutes.

Not more exit tweaking.

⸻

I would:
	1.	Revert to v6.6
	2.	Keep 15-min breathing
	3.	Keep 2-bar trail
	4.	Add early impulse requirement
	5.	Leave time_stop unchanged

Then re-test controlled window.
