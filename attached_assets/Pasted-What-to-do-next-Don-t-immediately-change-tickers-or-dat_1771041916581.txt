What to do next

Don’t immediately change tickers or date range yet. First, run one diagnostic that tells us which failure mode it is.

The single best diagnostic

For every trade, record the Maximum Favorable Excursion (MFE) in R within 30 minutes:

MFE_R = (max_high_after_entry - entryPrice) / riskPerShare for longs

also track time-to-MFE

Then look at:

% of trades that ever hit +0.5R

% that ever hit +1.0R

median time to +0.5R

Interpretation:

If most trades NEVER hit +0.5R → entries are fundamentally bad / levels not real / balance regime.

If many trades hit +0.5R but don’t close green → exit logic is killing you.

If trades hit +0.5R quickly but reverse → you’re buying exhaustion (late entry).

That one metric ends the guessing.

Parameter change I’d try (only if MFE confirms “late entry”)

If MFE shows you often get a quick pop then stall, fix timing:

That converts “dead trades” into tiny losses and protects expectancy.
