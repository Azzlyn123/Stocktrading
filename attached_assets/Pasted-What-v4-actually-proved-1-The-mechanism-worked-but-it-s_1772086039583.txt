What v4 actually proved

1) The mechanism worked (but it solved the wrong problem)
	•	Time-stop avg improved from –0.446R → –0.306R (good).
	•	Time-stop share increased (30% → 44%), meaning your conditional logic is firing more often and earlier.
So yes: you successfully cut grinders sooner.

2) The cost was unacceptable: you killed the right tail
	•	≥1R winners: v3 9.1% → v4 5.1%
	•	2R: v3 had 2, v4 has 0
	•	Avg winner fell: 0.80R → 0.56R
	•	StdDev fell: 0.695 → 0.546 (less spread = less tail)

This tells me the 0.5R exemption is too hard to earn, so trades that would have become 1R+ are now being classified as “not strong enough” and time-stopped.

3) The stop-hit worsening is a side effect of later BE lock

Stop-hit avg got worse (–0.188R → –0.322R) while stop-hit frequency fell.

That fits your explanation:
	•	If BE lock triggers later, fewer trades get protected.
	•	More trades revert through entry before protection engages, so when they do stop, it’s at worse R.

4) EOD deterioration is real but probably not the main story

EOD is small counts (13 vs 6) and can swing, but directionally it supports the same point: fewer runners.

The clean next move

You already said it: the right threshold is between 0.3R and 0.5R — but we can do better than “try 0.4R.”

v5 recommendation (best EV): two-tier exemption + earlier protection

Instead of a single hard exemption threshold:

A) Time-stop eligibility
	•	Apply time stop at 45m only if MFE < 0.30R
	•	This preserves the v3 behavior that allowed “early spark” trades to live.

B) Early protection (BE lock)
	•	Keep BE lock earlier than v4:
	•	Trigger BE lock at +0.30R MFE (or +0.35R if you want a small compromise)
	•	Then use your existing trailing/structure logic to manage.

C) Optional: “soft time stop” for MFE between 0.30R and 0.50R
	•	If MFE is 0.30R–0.50R at 45m:
	•	tighten stop to “structure minus small buffer” or to entry + tiny cushion (fees/slip)
	•	but do not hard exit immediately

Why this dominates v4:
	•	Grinders (MFE < 0.30R) still get chopped.
	•	Trades that show any real spark (0.30–0.50R) are allowed to mature into the right tail.
	•	BE protection engages earlier, improving the “stop hit” average.

My opinion: this is the highest-probability path to restoring v3’s tail while keeping some of v4’s grinder control.
