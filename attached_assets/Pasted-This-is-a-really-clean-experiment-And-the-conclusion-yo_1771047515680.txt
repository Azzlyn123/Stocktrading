This is a really clean experiment. And the conclusion you drew is the right one:

The exits were a leak, but the core issue is entries / regime.

When only ~21% of trades even validate to +0.3R, you don’t have a breakout engine — you have a “poke and stall” engine.

Also important: VALID+BE didn’t improve expectancy vs BE-only. That’s the tell that “fixing exits” is no longer the bottleneck.

So here’s where I’d go next.

What to focus on next
1) Fix the definition of a breakout level

Most “breakout+retest” systems fail because they break out of levels that aren’t real.

Common failure: using too many micro-levels from the same timeframe you trade (5m). Those levels get “tagged” constantly and create fake breakouts.

Upgrade:

Define levels on 15m or 30m, execute on 5m.

Only trade breakouts of major swing highs/lows, not minor pivot noise.

This alone often cuts dead trades massively.

2) Add a “range compression → expansion” setup requirement

Breakouts work best when they’re escaping compression.

Right now you’re catching random extensions.

Setup filter:

Require the 10-bar range to be tight before breakout:

e.g. range_last10 <= 0.8 * range_last30

and then breakout candle TR >= 1.5× avg TR10 (your expansion filter)

This makes it “coil then pop” instead of “random pop.”

3) Add a market regime gate that is momentum-based, not static

Static ATR floors won’t do it.

What you want is “is the overall tape moving right now?”

For longs:

SPY above VWAP and VWAP slope up

SPY making higher highs on 15m

OR SPY 5m EMA9 > EMA21 and slope positive

If SPY is flat/choppy, your breakout success rate collapses.

This is the #1 cause of low validation rates.

4) Fix friction realism separately (don’t let it confuse signal quality)

Your “stop at entry but still loses due to slippage” is realistic, but for diagnosing edge it’s muddy.

For the next test:

Keep slippage, but also report gross R before slippage vs net R after slippage.
If gross is still bad, entries are bad. If gross is decent and net is bad, execution model is too harsh.

The single highest-leverage next diagnostic

Before changing anything, run this:

Validation rate by session

What % validate within 3 bars during:

Open (first 60–90 min)

Midday

Power hour

If validation is concentrated in one session, your “edge” may exist only there.

Given your “spike then mean revert” behavior, I’d bet open/power hour are better than midday.

My recommendation for your next experiment

Do one change:

✅ Change breakout level timeframe to 15m/30m swings

Keep everything else identical (validation + BE stays).

Rerun the same date window.

If validation rate jumps from ~21% → ~35–45%, you’re on the right track.

If it stays ~20%, then the market regime filter is the bigger lever.
